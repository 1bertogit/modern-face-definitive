## What Are Jowls and Double Chin?

Jowls and double chin are aesthetic changes that appear with time due to a combination of factors, including genetics, loss of skin elasticity, and facial fat redistribution.

### Jowls: Jawline Sagging

Jowls manifest as sagging forming along the jawline, creating a "bulldog" appearance. This loss of definition results from decreased collagen and elastin, proteins supporting the skin.

Over time, gravity and bone volume loss also contribute to tissue drooping, accentuating jowls.

### Double Chin: Submental Fat Accumulation

Double chin, in turn, is characterized by fat accumulation in submental region, that is, below chin. This fat can be superficial (subcutaneous) or deep (subplatysmal), and its presence contributes to loss of angle between chin and neck.

Skin sagging and neck muscle weakness can also aggravate double chin appearance.

## Causes of Jowls and Double Chin

Understanding causes is fundamental to choose most suitable treatment and guarantee lasting results.

### Intrinsic Factors: Natural Aging

Aging is a complex process involving several changes in our facial anatomy:

*   **Collagen and elastin loss:** Reduces skin firmness and elasticity.

*   **Adipose tissue decrease:** Causes volume loss in areas like cheeks, accentuating sagging.

*   **Bone resorption:** Alters facial structural support, contributing to tissue drooping.

*   **Muscle weakening:** Affects facial contour support and definition.

### Extrinsic Factors: Habits and Lifestyle

Besides natural aging, some external factors can accelerate process:

*   **Sun exposure:** Damages collagen and elastin, accelerating skin aging.

*   **Smoking:** Impairs blood circulation and collagen production, compromising skin health.

*   **Inadequate diet:** A diet poor in essential nutrients can affect skin quality and collagen production.

*   **Sedentarism:** Lack of physical activity can contribute to muscle tone loss and fat accumulation.

### Genetics: A Family Predisposition

Genetic predisposition also plays an important role in development of jowls and double chin. Some people are naturally more prone to present sagging and fat accumulation in certain areas of face and neck.

## Surgical Treatments for Jowls and Double Chin: Restoring Facial Harmony

Although non-surgical treatments exist that can improve appearance of jowls and double chin, surgery offers more lasting and effective results, especially in cases of marked sagging and significant fat accumulation.

### Neck Lift: Redefining Neck Contour

The [neck lift](/en/blog/deep-neck-complete-guide), is a surgical procedure aiming to remove excess skin and fat, besides repositioning neck muscles, providing a more defined and rejuvenated contour.

There are different neck lift techniques, which can be combined to meet specific needs of each patient. Main ones include:

*   **Platysmaplasty:** Suture or plication of platysma muscle to reduce sagging and improve neck contour.

*   **Liposuction:** Removal of excess fat in submental region (double chin).

*   **Skin resection:** Removal of excess skin to eliminate sagging and improve contour definition.

### Rhytidectomy: Rejuvenating Lower Third of Face

Rhytidectomy, or facelift, is a surgical procedure aiming to reposition facial tissues, smoothing wrinkles, folds, and sagging that appear with aging.

In context of treating jowls and double chin, rhytidectomy can be used to:

*   **Redefine jawline:** Reposition facial tissues, attenuating jowls sagging and restoring a more defined contour.

*   **Correct facial volume:** Rhytidectomy allows facial fat redistribution, filling depressed areas and smoothing folds and wrinkles.

*   **Improve neck appearance:** In many cases, rhytidectomy is combined with neck lift to provide a more harmonious and comprehensive result.

### Deep Neck: Deep Treatment for Lasting Results

[Deep neck](/en/blog/deep-neck-complete-guide) is an advanced technique aiming to remodel deep neck structures, providing more lasting and natural results. Unlike traditional neck lift, which acts mainly on skin and platysma muscle, deep neck addresses:

*   **Subplatysmal fat:** Removes excess fat located below platysma muscle, thinning the neck.

*   **Digastric muscle:** Modifies digastric muscle, responsible for neck contour.

*   **Cervical fascia:** Treats cervical fascia, a tissue layer surrounding neck structures, providing greater support and definition.

## Risks and Complications: Transparent Information for Your Safety

Like every surgical procedure, jowls and double chin surgery presents some potential risks and complications. It is important to be aware of them and discuss all your doubts with Dr. Robério Brandão before making a decision.

Some of most common risks and complications include:

*   **Hematoma:** Blood accumulation under skin.

*   **Seroma:** Fluid accumulation under skin.

*   **Infection:** Rare, but can occur.

*   **Sensitivity alteration:** Numbness or tingling may occur in operated area, usually temporary.

*   **Hypertrophic scars or keloids:** Raised and thick scars.

*   **Nerve injury:** Rare, but can cause temporary or permanent muscle weakness or paralysis.

*   **Asymmetry:** Some asymmetry in facial contour may occur.

Dr. Robério Brandão adopts all necessary measures to minimize these risks, including careful pre-operative evaluation, precise surgical technique, and rigorous post-operative follow-up.

## What to Expect: Recovery Timeline

Recovery is a journey requiring patience and specific care in each phase. Knowing what to expect helps reduce anxiety and optimize results.

### First 24-48 Hours

This is the most delicate period. It is normal to present:

- **Moderate to intense edema**: swelling is maximum in this period
- **Bruising**: purple spots may appear and are a natural part of healing
- **Manageable discomfort**: pain is generally mild to moderate, controlled with prescribed painkillers
- **Need for absolute rest**: avoid sudden movements and keep head elevated

### First Week (Days 3-7)

From the third day, you will start noticing progressive improvements:

- Edema begins to decrease gradually
- Bruises change color (purple → green → yellow)
- Stitches and dressings may be removed as per medical advice
- Light activities can be resumed with caution

### Weeks 2-4

Important transition phase:

- Gradual return to social and professional activities
- Residual edema still present, but less noticeable
- Result begins to define itself, although not yet definitive
- Lymphatic drainage massages may be indicated

### Months 1-3

Final result consolidates progressively:

- Tissues settle into their definitive position
- Scars mature and become less visible
- Sensitivity returns gradually to normal
- Final result visible between 3-6 months

## Post-Operative Warning Signs

Knowing warning signs allows early identification of any complication and quick action.

### When to Seek Surgeon Immediately

Some signs require urgent medical evaluation:

- **Active bleeding** that does not stop with light compression for 10-15 minutes
- **Fever** above 38°C (100.4°F), especially if accompanied by chills
- **Intense and sudden pain** not controlled with prescribed medication
- **Abrupt asymmetry** — much larger swelling on one side may indicate hematoma
- **Skin color change** — paleness or darkening in specific areas
- **Purulent discharge** with unpleasant odor

### Normal Signs (Do Not Worry)

These symptoms are expected and do not require concern:

- **Moderate edema** in the first days, especially in the morning
- **Bruising** (purple spots) changing color progressively
- **Tingling or numbness** temporary in operated area
- **Tightness sensation** as tissues heal
- **Small temporary asymmetries** due to irregular edema
- **Difficulty smiling** or moving face in first days

### Safety Protocol

In case of doubt, follow this protocol:

1. **Document** — take photos and note symptoms
2. **Contact** — call surgeon's team
3. **Do not self-medicate** — avoid non-prescribed medications
4. **Stay calm** — most complications are manageable
