faq:
  - question: "Can fillers replace facelift?"
    answer: "Not completely. Fillers add volume and can camouflage grooves, but don't reposition ptosed tissues. In cases of significant ptosis, excessive filler creates a 'swollen' appearance without resolving the structural problem."
  - question: "When do fillers become insufficient?"
    answer: "When there's significant tissue ptosis (descended malar, jowl, platysmal bands), when increasing volumes of filler are needed to maintain results, or when the face is losing definition despite treatments."
  - question: "Can I get fillers before deciding on facelift?"
    answer: "Yes, fillers can be a step along the way. Many patients start with injectables and eventually opt for surgery when fillers are no longer sufficient. The important thing is not to overdo volume."
  - question: "Does facelift eliminate the need for fillers?"
    answer: "Often reduces but doesn't always eliminate. Facelift repositions tissues; fillers add volume. After facelift, less filler is needed, but some patients still benefit from supplementation in specific areas."
  - question: "Is it true that too much filler makes facelift difficult?"
    answer: "Yes. Biostimulators and excessive filler volumes can create fibrosis and alter anatomical planes, making surgical dissection difficult. The Modern Face technique was developed to deal with this reality."
  - question: "What's the long-term cost-benefit?"
    answer: "Fillers require frequent maintenance (6-18 months). After 5-10 years of regular fillers, accumulated cost often exceeds that of a facelift lasting 8-12 years. Analysis should be individualized."
  - question: "I'm 45 â€” facelift or fillers?"
    answer: "Depends on anatomy, not age. At 45, if there's significant ptosis, facelift may be indicated. If there's volume loss without ptosis, fillers may be sufficient. Evaluation is individual."
  - question: "Can I do both?"
    answer: "Yes, and it's often the best approach. Facelift corrects ptosis and fillers (in smaller quantities post-facelift) add volume where needed. Treatments are complementary, not competitive."
