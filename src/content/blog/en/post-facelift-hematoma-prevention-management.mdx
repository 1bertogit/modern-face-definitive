# Post-Facelift Hematoma: Essential Prevention and Management

Post-facelift hematoma is a complication that, although infrequent in experienced hands, can cause concern and impact patient recovery. Understanding its causes, prevention methods, and treatment protocols is crucial to ensure a smooth post-operative period and optimize your facelift results.

In this article, Dr. Robério Brandão, creator of the [Modern Face](/en/blog/modern-face-concept-philosophy) philosophy and specialist in minimally invasive techniques, shares his knowledge and experience to help surgeons prevent and manage post-facelift hematomas, ensuring the safety and satisfaction of their patients.

---

## What is a Post-Facelift Hematoma?

A hematoma is, in simple terms, a collection of blood outside blood vessels, usually under the skin. After a facelift, a procedure involving tissue repositioning and, inevitably, blood vessel manipulation, small bleedings can occur.

This extravasated blood accumulates in tissue spaces, resulting in:

- Swelling in the operated area
- Pain and tenderness to touch
- Skin discoloration, ranging from red to purple and eventually to greenish or yellowish

[Image: Post-facelift hematoma - showing different discoloration stages]

It is important to emphasize that the formation of a small hematoma is not necessarily a sign of surgical error. However, proper management is fundamental to avoid complications and ensure optimized recovery.

---

## Common Causes of Post-Facelift Hematoma

Hematoma formation after a facelift can be multifactorial. The most common causes include:

1. **Rupture of small blood vessels during surgery:** Even with meticulous surgical techniques, tissue manipulation can lead to small vessel rupture.

2. **Coagulation problems:** Patients with pre-existing coagulation disorders or using anticoagulant medications (even natural ones) have a higher predisposition to bleeding and hematomas.

3. **Blood pressure spikes:** Sudden blood pressure increases in the post-operative period, whether from physical effort, coughing, or vomiting, can lead to the rupture of fragile vessels.

4. **Inadequate surgical technique:** Excessively aggressive dissection or lack of hemostasis (bleeding control) during surgery can increase hematoma risk.

It is crucial for the surgeon to be attentive to these factors and adopt preventive measures to minimize hematoma risk.

## Detailed Hematoma Prevention Protocol

### 1. Thorough Pre-Operative Evaluation

A complete patient evaluation is the first step to minimize risks. This includes:

- **Detailed medical history:** Investigating presence of coagulation disorders, use of anticoagulant medications, or history of abnormal bleeding.
- **Laboratory tests:** Requesting tests to evaluate coagulation function and identify possible alterations.
- **Medication and supplement suspension:** Instructing patient to suspend use of anticoagulant medications (like aspirin) and supplements that may increase bleeding risk (like vitamin E, ginkgo biloba, and garlic) at least two weeks before surgery.
- **Blood pressure control:** Ensuring patient has controlled blood pressure before surgery.

### 2. Meticulous Surgical Technique

The surgical technique employed plays a crucial role in hematoma prevention. Dr. Robério Brandão uses minimally invasive techniques and emphasizes:

- **Careful dissection:** Delicate tissue manipulation to minimize blood vessel injury.
- **Rigorous hemostasis:** Meticulous bleeding control during surgery, using bipolar electrocautery to seal small vessels.
- **Strategic planning:** Avoiding areas of higher bleeding risk, like regions with high blood vessel concentration.
- **Direct Vision Techniques:** Using the [Endomidface](/en/blog/endomidface-complete-guide) technique by Direct Vision, created by Dr. Robério Brandão, allows clearer visualization of blood vessels, facilitating hemostasis and minimizing injury risk.

"The secret to avoiding hematomas is not operating faster, but operating with more precision and care." – Dr. Robério Brandão

### 3. Intra and Post-Operative Blood Pressure Control

Keeping blood pressure under control is fundamental to prevent bleeding and hematomas. This involves:

- **Continuous monitoring:** Rigorous blood pressure tracking during surgery.
- **Medication:** Using antihypertensive medications, if necessary, to maintain blood pressure at adequate levels.
- **Post-operative instructions:** Instructing patient to avoid activities that may raise blood pressure, such as intense physical effort and Valsalva maneuvers (holding breath and straining).

### 4. Specific Medication

Dr. Robério Brandão uses specific medications to aid in hematoma prevention:

- **Tranexamic acid:** This medication helps stabilize blood clots and reduce bleeding. It is administered intravenously during surgery and later orally in post-operative.
- **Corticosteroids:** Reduce inflammation and edema, aiding recovery and decreasing hematoma risk.

### 5. Adequate Post-Operative Care

Post-operative care is as important as surgical technique in hematoma prevention. Guidelines include:

- **Rest:** Recommending relative rest in first days after surgery, avoiding activities that may increase blood pressure.
- **Cold compresses:** Applying cold compresses to operated area in first 48 hours to reduce swelling and bleeding.
- **Head elevation:** Keeping head elevated during sleep to decrease edema.
- **Compressive dressings:** Using light compressive dressings to aid hemostasis and reduce swelling.
- **Lymphatic drainage:** Performing manual lymphatic drainage sessions to aid in excess fluid removal and reduce edema.

## Hematoma Management: What to Do If It Happens

Even with all preventive measures, formation of a small hematoma can still occur. Adequate management is essential to minimize impact and avoid complications.

### 1. Early Identification

It is crucial that the patient takes instruction to monitor the operated area and report any sign of hematoma, such as:

- Increased swelling
- Intense pain
- Tenderness
- Skin discoloration

### 2. Conservative Treatment

In most cases, small hematomas can be treated conservatively with:

- **Cold compresses:** Applying cold compresses for 20 minutes every hour in first 48 hours.
- **Rest:** Avoiding activities that may increase blood pressure.
- **Pain medication:** Using painkillers prescribed by doctor to relieve pain.

### 3. Surgical Drainage

In more severe cases, when hematoma is large, causes tissue compression, or presents signs of infection, surgical drainage may be necessary.

- **Procedure:** Surgeon performs a small incision in skin to drain accumulated blood.
- **Antibiotics:** In case of infection, antibiotics will be prescribed.

[Image: Hematoma surgical drainage procedure]

### 4. Continuous Monitoring

After treatment, it is important to monitor patient to ensure hematoma resolution and identify early any sign of complication.

---

## Complications of an Untreated Hematoma

Although most hematomas resolve spontaneously, lack of adequate treatment can lead to complications, including:

- **Infection:** Blood accumulation can serve as culture medium for bacteria.
- **Fibrosis:** Hematoma can lead to formation of fibrous scar tissue, resulting in skin irregularities.
- **Skin necrosis:** In extreme cases, blood vessel compression by hematoma can lead to skin necrosis.
- **Aesthetic result compromise:** Fibrosis and skin irregularities can compromise final facelift result.

## What to Expect: Recovery Timeline

Recovery is a journey requiring patience and specific care in each phase. Knowing what to expect helps reduce anxiety and optimize results.

### First 24-48 Hours

This is the most delicate period. It is normal to present:

- **Moderate to intense edema**: swelling is maximum in this period
- **Bruising**: purple spots may appear and are a natural part of healing
- **Manageable discomfort**: pain is generally mild to moderate, controlled with prescribed painkillers
- **Need for absolute rest**: avoid sudden movements and keep head elevated

### First Week (Days 3-7)

From the third day, you will start noticing progressive improvements:

- Edema begins to decrease gradually
- Bruises change color (purple → green → yellow)
- Stitches and dressings may be removed as per medical advice
- Light activities can be resumed with caution

### Weeks 2-4

Important transition phase:

- Gradual return to social and professional activities
- Residual edema still present, but less noticeable
- Result begins to define itself, although not yet definitive
- Lymphatic drainage massages may be indicated

### Months 1-3

Final result consolidates progressively:

- Tissues settle into their definitive position
- Scars mature and become less visible
- Sensitivity returns gradually to normal
- Final result visible between 3-6 months

> "Patience is fundamental. The final result of a facial procedure can only be evaluated after 6 months, when all tissues have already stabilized." — Dr. Robério Brandão

## Post-Operative Warning Signs

Knowing warning signs allows early identification of any complication and quick action.

### When to Seek Surgeon Immediately

Some signs require urgent medical evaluation:

- **Active bleeding** that does not stop with light compression for 10-15 minutes
- **Fever** above 38°C (100.4°F), especially if accompanied by chills
- **Intense and sudden pain** not controlled with prescribed medication
- **Abrupt asymmetry** — much larger swelling on one side may indicate hematoma
- **Skin color change** — paleness or darkening in specific areas
- **Purulent discharge** with unpleasant odor

### Normal Signs (Do Not Worry)

These symptoms are expected and do not require concern:

- **Moderate edema** in the first days, especially in the morning
- **Bruising** (purple spots) changing color progressively
- **Tingling or numbness** temporary in operated area
- **Tightness sensation** as tissues heal
- **Small temporary asymmetries** due to irregular edema
- **Difficulty smiling** or moving face in first days

### Safety Protocol

In case of doubt, follow this protocol:

1. **Document** — take photos and note symptoms
2. **Contact** — call surgeon's team
3. **Do not self-medicate** — avoid non-prescribed medications
4. **Stay calm** — most complications are manageable

> "In Modern Face, each patient receives a direct communication channel for emergencies. Safety is not a luxury, it is a priority." — Dr. Robério Brandão

## Detailed Post-Operative Care

Procedure success depends as much on surgical technique as on post-operative care.

### First Days: Critical Phase

In this phase, small cares make big difference:

- **Elevated rest**: keep head elevated at 30-45° even when sleeping
- **Cold compresses**: apply in first 48h to reduce edema
- **Rigorous medication**: follow exactly prescribed schedules
- **Light diet**: prefer soft foods and avoid excessive chewing
- **Abundant hydration**: water helps in tissue recovery

### First Week: Intermediate Care

As healing advances:

- **Careful hygiene**: clean area as directed, without rubbing
- **Avoid sun exposure**: use hat and sunscreen when necessary to go out
- **Gentle facial movements**: avoid exaggerated expressions
- **Adequate sleep**: body recovers mainly during sleep
- **Avoid alcohol and tobacco**: both harm healing

### Weeks 2-4: Gradual Return

Return to activities should be progressive:

- **Light activities**: short walks are allowed
- **Remote work**: can be resumed as per comfort
- **Light makeup**: generally allowed after 2 weeks
- **Moderate exercises**: only with medical clearance
- **Lymphatic drainage**: indicated to accelerate recovery

### What to Avoid

During entire recovery period:

- Direct sun exposure on operated areas
- Intense physical activities before clearance
- Alcohol consumption in first 2 weeks
- Smoking (ideally stop 4 weeks before and after)
- Non-prescribed anti-inflammatories
- Sudden movements with face
